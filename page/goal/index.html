2. Define the Functionality

Find out what do you want to do

Once you have your guideline, the next step is to understand how to accomplish such goal and identify the necessary functions that is required for the desired robot design. In Micromouse, the sensor is detecting with infrared intensity. The system control is done with a wheel encoder. The decision making is processed inside the microcontroller.

3. Research on the Components and Devices

List Out The Essential Components

The same function can be found through multiple devices and IC chips, so it is your job to read through the data sheet of each of those components and decide the best fit product for your design. This process usually takes much time, but it is a very important step which will significantly affect the performance of the robot. The components will determine the limitation of the robot and it will be extremely difficult to change the specification in the future.

4. Coordinate Every Components

List Out The Essential Components

After know what components to use for your design, the second most important step is to make sure every of those chosen components will work with each other. Sometimes, each component can function properly by itself, but they might conflict with other parts while they are assembled together. The main task here is to test them all together and ensure the system can generate the desired output.

5. Layout the Circuit with PCB

List Out The Essential Components

This step will finalize the hardware design, and any mistake made here will be irreversible, so you should proceed to this step with extra cautious. In my case, Eagle CAD is the software that I used for PCB layout. The recommended circuit schematic for each components can be fund in its data sheet, it is very important to study the data sheet before using the device. The more detail of fabricating PCB can be found under the “Learn More” tab.

6. Assemble the hardware

Finally, you have finished your design, the last step is to solder the IC chip to the PCB and then secure other required hardware pieces. Then, the robot is ready for programming, and the coding process is just a free play ground where you can apply any functionality to the robot as long as the function fits in the hardware limitation.
